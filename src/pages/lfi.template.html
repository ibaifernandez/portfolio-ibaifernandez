Proof module / POCURO

Client snapshot
POCURO

Chilean real-estate developer whose acquisition engine had been optimized for volume, not for buyer readiness or sales usability.

Within my LFi scope, POCURO was generating more than 60,000 monthly leads from Meta and Google Ads, but fewer than 5% showed real purchase intent. The problem was not demand. It was an acquisition machine optimized for quantity that the sales team could not process efficiently.

That made the job concrete: reduce low-value interactions, rebalance spend toward higher-intent channels, automate first-line qualification, and hand sales a structured view of buyer intent instead of raw noise.

POCURO DIDN'T NEED MORE LEADS. IT NEEDED A BETTER FILTER.

Intervention sequence

The POCURO intervention did not begin with a prettier funnel. It began by separating raw acquisition volume from sales-ready demand and making that difference operationally visible.

Lead mix Meta volume vs. higher-intent search
Starting state 60k+ monthly leads, under 5% buyer intent
Reported outcome 500 high-intent buyers routed monthly
  1. 01

    Rebalance the acquisition mix

    Channel strategy moved away from brute-force volume once higher-intent search demand proved commercially healthier than Meta-heavy capture alone.

  2. 02

    Automate first-line qualification

    A WhatsApp chatbot built with n8n and a custom knowledge base absorbed the repetitive filtering work before a human sales touch was required.

  3. 03

    Score intent instead of guessing it

    Lead scoring created a visible split between raw demand, manageable qualified pool, and buyers who actually deserved downstream attention.

  4. 04

    Route cleaner outputs to sales

    The result was not just fewer leads. It was structured, prioritized handoff that let the sales layer work on signal instead of noise.

What changed operationally

  • Meta-heavy acquisition was rebalanced toward higher-intent search demand.
  • WhatsApp chatbot flows absorbed repetitive qualification work before human follow-up.
  • Lead scoring created a visible split between raw capture and real buyer readiness.
  • Sales stopped drowning in volume and started receiving structured, better-prioritized opportunities.

Proof module / Leben

Client snapshot
Leben

Chilean real-estate brand at ileben.cl whose email operation had accumulated deliverability debt, list noise, and weak conversion performance despite a large contact base.

Within my coordination scope at LFi, the Leben intervention combined technical reputation recovery, list hygiene, ESP migration, and conversion-focused email UX.

The important point is not authorship theatre. It is that the repair followed an explicit operating sequence and turned a noisy, risky base into a healthier commercial channel.

LEBEN SHOWED THAT DELIVERABILITY IS A REVENUE PROBLEM.

Intervention sequence

Inside the area I coordinated, the Leben repair followed four operating moves before the performance lift became visible in the numbers.

Public surface ileben.cl
Starting state 69k contacts under reputation pressure
Reported outcome 99.66% delivery rate
  1. 01

    Stabilize sender trust

    Blacklist recovery, IP warm-up, and SPF, DKIM, and DMARC hardened the infrastructure before scale resumed.

  2. 02

    Cut the base to a usable core

    SQL cleanup, bounce removal, and activity filters reduced a noisy 69k-contact base into a list the channel could actually serve.

  3. 03

    Re-platform the send layer

    The Mailchimp to Brevo migration lowered fragility and gave the team a cleaner, more governable operating surface.

  4. 04

    Redesign for response

    Mobile-first email UX, WhatsApp entry points, and send-time learning made the channel commercially useful again.
